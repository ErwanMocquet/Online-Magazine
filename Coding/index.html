An Animated History





Nowadays, everybody have heard about animation. With Disney, animes or even Netflix, the animated stories are all around us. One thing that the public may know less is where does that come from and how did it evolved? That's what we're going to try to explain to you through this article.










Contrary to what you can think, Disney is not the first company that have created the first "animation movie". The first one, named "Fantasmagorie" has been release by the Gaumont Studios company and directed by Emile COHL on the 17th of august 1908 in Paris. It was a short animation showing a stickman interacting with a few objects.
After "Fantasmagorie", the animation design sector has been mainly led by Disney fron the beginning of the 1920' with their first movie release named "Steamboat Willie". From that time on, Disney have been the leader in the world of animation of cartoons/movies.







Throughout the years until now, the animation has been developed in many other fields than movies:







First, video games have taken a huge part of the development and the progress in this sector. From Pong to the last Triple-A games (games produced and diffused by major publishers), a very long journey has been undertaken.










Then, with the explosion of Japanese culture during the 1970’/80’, anime cartoons and japanese styled animations became enormously popular, and indeed, many became household names.










Finally, we are finding some of those animations in series as in Love, Death & Robots broadcasted on Netflix or more lately, the MCU series What If…? Animated series are today everywhere, from the Japanese anime to the lastest American series, we have a very wide range of choices.
