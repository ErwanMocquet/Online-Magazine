2. STOP MOTION STYLE



The stop motion style is clearly less known than Disney style. However, it doesn't make is less good. For a quick explaination, the stop motion style is a filmmaking technique in which objects are physically manipulated in small increments between individually photographed frames so that they will appear to exhibit independent motion or change when the series of frames is played back. This style is clearly one of the most under-rated by the public.



The most famous director who works (almost) always with stop motion is Wes Anderson. Aside of his movies made in stop motion, Wes is also known for "The Grand Budapest Hotel" or "The Royal Tenenbaums".



Wes had made two movies in stop motion, "Fantastic Mr. Fox" (2009) and "Isle Of Dogs" (2018). Both where nominated for the Academy Award For Best Animated Feature. Aside from Mr. Anderson, another movie which came out in 1993 made a lot of noise. Which one? "The Nightmare Before Christmas" of course, directed at the time by Tim Burton and Henry Selick.





3. JAPANESE STYLE



The japanese style isn't the most known style neither, even if it developped quite well in some country of Europe during the 80' and 90'. When we say Japanese style it doesn't really describe any type as all the movies/animes that came from the Asian continent are very different. However, we always find the same kind of cartoonish style for the mangas.



The probably most famous Japanese director known around the world is Hayao Miyazaki. He is the one behind the Studio Ghibli and gave to the world a lot of very famous movies. He's also known to put a second level of reading in his movie, often refering to the environment, pacifism, feminism, love or even family.



Miyazaki is the one that gave us some masterpiece like "My Neighbour Totoro"(1988) that treats about animism, enivronment and the joy of rural living. "Princess Momonoke" (1997) which is about environmentalism as well and how the humans are destroying the planet. Or even "Spirited Away" (2001) that treats about environmentalism as well but also about Western consumerism and death.
